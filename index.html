Project Synopsis

Project Synopsis
Runner-up of the 2017 Target Hackathon -- Designing a Mobile Application for Left Behind Children in China. Awarded second-place prize of ¥10000. The idea of the application is to facilitate and encourage communication between left behind children and their migrant parents through sending multimedia greeting cards -- these cards can have handwriting, photos, videos, and audio attached. An animated paper crane delivers these cards and encourages continued dialogue between the child and the parent.

Project Synopsis
Fifteen solenoids connected using an Arduino Mega are used to drive a robotic glockenspiel that a user can control wirelessly with their smartphone. The glockenspiel can be controlled via a virtual keyboard, a beatpad, or a force touch pressure sensitive display. The pressure sensitive mode can create ordered chaos, a new way of experiencing the sounds that a glockenspiel can make. It can also play pre-programmed songs. The songs in the demo are: Twinkle Twinkle, Jingle Bells, Mozart K330, Canon, Chao Ming.
